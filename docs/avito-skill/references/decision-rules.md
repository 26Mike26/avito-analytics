# Decision Rules

Use account-relative baselines instead of hardcoded universal thresholds where possible. Compare a listing to peer listings in the same account, category, location, price band, and recent date range.

## Listing Diagnosis

- High views with low contacts usually points to price, title, photos, location, category, delivery terms, or offer mismatch.
- High contacts with low order or sale progress usually points to weak response speed, seller process, product fit, unavailable stock, delivery terms, or trust concerns.
- Low impressions on a profitable listing makes the listing a promotion candidate.
- Stale inventory needs price, content, category, stock, or availability review before more promotion.
- Price-change candidates should be supported by market data, conversion data, inventory age, and margin sensitivity.
- Listings with missing photos, weak titles, unclear attributes, stock risk, or policy/quality issues should be improved before paid promotion.

## Promotion Diagnosis

- Evaluate promotion against marginal contacts, leads, CPA actions, orders, or revenue, not views alone.
- High spend with low contacts means pause, reduce, or rework before more promotion.
- Strong organic conversion can justify paid scaling when stock, margin, and fulfillment capacity are healthy.
- Out-of-stock or operationally risky listings should not be promoted.
- Prefer a lower-budget test when conversion is plausible but confidence is limited.
- Use forecasts, suggests, bids, current orders, and account spend history before recommending paid execution.

## Lead Leakage

- Old unread chats and missed calls are lead leakage.
- Many leads on a few listings may justify promotion, stock protection, faster response routing, or stronger seller scripts.
- Contact volume without progress suggests process friction, weak qualification, delivery barriers, or poor response timing.
- CPA complaint candidates should be supported by action IDs, reason, evidence, and SDK-read data.

## Orders And Stock

- Orders missing tracking or seller action are immediate operational risks.
- Return orders needing acceptance should be separated from routine fulfillment tasks.
- Stock mismatches can make promotion wasteful and damage buyer trust.
- Listings with low or zero stock should be excluded from promotion recommendations unless replenishment is confirmed.

## Reviews And Reputation

- Negative reviews without answers are reputation risks.
- Draft responses should be specific, factual, polite, and avoid admitting unsupported fault.
- Repeated review themes should become operational actions, not only response templates.

## Realty

- Price above market correspondence needs price correction or stronger positioning.
- Booking-calendar gaps can indicate price, availability, seasonal, or listing-quality issues.
- Seasonal price periods should be tied to demand, occupancy, and local market signals.

## Jobs

- Vacancy health depends on views, responses, application states, candidate fit, freshness, and tariff constraints.
- Applications needing action should be prioritized by age, state, and candidate relevance.
- Resume contacts should be used only where the workflow and user intent justify contacting candidates.

## Account And Team

- Balance and operations history should explain whether spend is intentional, anomalous, or constrained.
- Employee ownership of listings helps diagnose slow responses, listing quality variance, and operational accountability.
- Tariff constraints can explain missing features, limits, or unexpected paid-service availability.
